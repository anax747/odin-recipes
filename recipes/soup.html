Vegetable Soup

Vegetable soup is a wholesome and hearty dish made with a variety of fresh vegetables simmered in a flavorful broth, often seasoned with herbs and spices for a comforting and nutritious meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 medium carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 medium potato, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup green beans, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 cup peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes (with juice)
  • 6 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup chopped kale or spinach (optional)
  • 1/2 cup pasta or rice, cooked separately (optional)
  • 1 can (15 oz) beans, such as cannellini, kidney, or chickpeas, drained and rinsed (optional)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional, for garnish)

Steps:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  2. Add the diced onion and saut√© for about 5 minutes until translucent.
  3. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the carrots, celery, and potatoes. Cook for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Add the green beans, zucchini, corn, and peas. Stir to combine.
  6. Pour in the vegetable broth and diced tomatoes (with juice). Stir well.
  7. Add the dried thyme, basil, oregano, and bay leaf. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 20-25 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  9. If using chopped kale or spinach, add it in the last 5 minutes of cooking.
  10. If adding pasta or rice, cook them separately and add just before serving.
  11. If using canned beans, add them during the last 10 minutes of cooking to heat through.
  12. Once the vegetables are cooked, taste the soup and adjust the seasoning as needed. Remove the bay leaf before serving.
  13. Serve the soup hot, garnished with fresh parsley and grated Parmesan cheese if desired.
